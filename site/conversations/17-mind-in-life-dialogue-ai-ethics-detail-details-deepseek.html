Core Theme & Purpose

The dialogue explores the potential of artificial intelligence (AI) to alleviate suffering, advance equity, and support human and planetary flourishing, while also examining the risks it poses to health, education, work, politics, and climate.

At stake is not only how AI evolves, but how human beings shape its trajectory through ethical qualities and agency.

It brings together leading scientists, scholars, contemplative practitioners, business leaders, and policymakers to examine the understanding of the living mind and the evolving relationship between human and machine intelligence.

The event draws on diverse wisdom and knowledge traditions to ask how compassion, responsibility, and ethical discernment can guide the development and use of emerging technologies.

Five Key Topics & Generative Questions (explored across the three days):

1. Minds — What is intelligence? What distinguishes living minds from artificial minds?

2. (Dis)entanglement and meaningful relationships — How are human minds intimately entangled with artificial minds? What ethical considerations emerge? What are the potential benefits?

4. Diversity and ethics — How might a more complex understanding of intelligence impact the AI systems we develop and the regulations we put in place? Is it possible to embed ethical values into AI?

5. Education — What are the potential risks of AI to young people? How is AI shaping the youngest minds in terms of attention, concentration, cognitive biases, and emotion regulation?

The Living Value Accounting System (LVAS)


A Framework for Calculating the True Worth of Living Systems


---


I. THE CORE PRINCIPLE


"A living tree's value is greater than a dead tree's price."


The LVAS replaces single-value extraction accounting (what can we take?) with multi-value flourishing accounting (what does this life give?).


It calculates value across five interconnected capitals:


Capital Type What It Measures Current Market Status

Ecological Capital Carbon capture, water filtration, oxygen production, soil formation, biodiversity habitat Almost entirely invisible

Community Capital Cultural significance, spiritual value, intergenerational knowledge, place-based identity Completely invisible

Health Capital Clean air, disease regulation, mental health benefits, temperature regulation Barely recognized

Resilience Capital Flood prevention, drought mitigation, climate stabilization, crop pollination Only noticed when lost

Economic Capital Timber, non-timber products, eco-tourism, sustainable harvest The ONLY thing currently counted


---


II. THE FORMULA


For any living system (a forest, a wetland, a mangrove, a prairie), the Living Value Index (LVI) is calculated as:


```

LVI = (EC + CC + HC + RC) x SF


Where:

EC = Ecological Capital Value

CC = Community Capital Value

HC = Health Capital Value

RC = Resilience Capital Value

SF = Scalability Factor (how irreplaceable is this specific ecosystem?)

```

Sub-Component Calculation Method Example Value (per hectare of mature forest/year)

One of the biggest problems in reforestation: trees die after planting because no one is paid to care for them.


Sweat Tokens solve this:


Problem Sweat Token Solution

"I planted my trees, now I'm done." Tokens vest over time. You only get full value if the trees survive. Partial payout if they die.

"Why should I protect someone else's forest?" If you planted it, you own a piece of its value. Your wealth rises as the forest thrives.

"The community doesn't benefit from carbon credits." Carbon buyers can purchase Sweat Tokens directly, paying the workers, not just middlemen.

"Elders have knowledge but can't do heavy labor." Elders are paid verification tokens for overseeing and blessing the work. Their wisdom is valued.
