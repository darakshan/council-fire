Event Title2025 Mind & Life Dialogue XXXIX: Minds, Artificial Intelligence, and Ethics

Dates & LocationOctober 14, 15, and 16, 2025 Broadcast live from the Dalai Lama Library and Archives, Dharamsala, India

Core Theme & PurposeThe dialogue explores the potential of artificial intelligence (AI) to alleviate suffering, advance equity, and support human and planetary flourishing, while also examining the risks it poses to health, education, work, politics, and climate. At stake is not only how AI evolves, but how human beings shape its trajectory through ethical qualities and agency. It brings together leading scientists, scholars, contemplative practitioners, business leaders, and policymakers to examine the understanding of the living mind and the evolving relationship between human and machine intelligence. The event draws on diverse wisdom and knowledge traditions to ask how compassion, responsibility, and ethical discernment can guide the development and use of emerging technologies.

Five Key Topics & Generative Questions (explored across the three days):

  1. Minds — What is intelligence? What distinguishes living minds from artificial minds?
  2. (Dis)entanglement and meaningful relationships — How are human minds intimately entangled with artificial minds? What ethical considerations emerge? What are the potential benefits?
  3. Collective narratives and possible futures — How do the stories we tell about AI create self-fulfilling prophecies? What are the risks to human survival and the possible replacement of human rationality and awareness?
  4. Diversity and ethics — How might a more complex understanding of intelligence impact the AI systems we develop and the regulations we put in place? Is it possible to embed ethical values into AI?
  5. Education — What are the potential risks of AI to young people? How is AI shaping the youngest minds in terms of attention, concentration, cognitive biases, and emotion regulation?
